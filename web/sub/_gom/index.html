Introduction: The Observer Problem

The Unresolved Questions of Modern Physics

The dual triumphs of General Relativity and the Standard Model of particle physics represent one of the greatest intellectual achievements in human history. Yet, their very success has brought the remaining mysteries into sharper relief. A complete theory of physics must contend with several profound and unanswered questions:

  • The Nature of Dark Energy: The source of the universe's accelerating expansion remains a complete mystery.
  • The Measurement Problem: Why does a quantum system "collapse" into a single, definite state upon measurement?
  • The Thermodynamic Arrow of Time: Why does the macroscopic world exhibit a clear "arrow of time" when fundamental laws are time-symmetric?
  • The Fine-Tuning Problem: Why are the fundamental constants of nature precisely calibrated for the existence of life?
